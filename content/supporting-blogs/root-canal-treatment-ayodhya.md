## What Is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment (RCT) is a dental procedure used to treat infection or damage inside a tooth. The inner portion of the tooth, called the pulp, contains nerves and blood vessels. When this pulp becomes infected due to deep decay, cracks, or trauma, a root canal is needed to save the tooth.

During the procedure:

* The infected pulp is removed
* The root canals are cleaned and disinfected
* The space is sealed to prevent reinfection
* A crown is usually placed to restore strength

Contrary to common myths, modern root canal treatment is comfortable and highly effective.

## When Do You Need Root Canal Treatment?

Not all tooth pain requires RCT, but certain symptoms strongly indicate the need for it.

### Common Signs You May Need a Root Canal

* Persistent toothache
* Sensitivity to hot or cold that lasts
* Swelling or tenderness in gums
* Darkening of the tooth
* Pain while chewing or biting

If these symptoms are ignored, the infection can spread, leading to tooth loss or more serious complications.

## Why Choose Root Canal Treatment Instead of Tooth Extraction?

Many patients wonder whether it’s better to remove the tooth instead. Dentists strongly recommend saving the natural tooth whenever possible.

### Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

* Preserves your natural tooth
* Maintains proper chewing and bite
* Prevents shifting of nearby teeth
* Avoids the need for tooth replacement
* Provides long-term relief from pain

Saving your tooth with RCT is often more cost-effective and healthier than extraction followed by replacement.

## Root Canal Treatment Procedure Explained Simply

Understanding the steps helps reduce anxiety and builds confidence.

### Step 1: Diagnosis and X-Ray

The dentist examines the tooth and takes digital X-rays to assess the extent of infection.

### Step 2: Local Anesthesia

The area is numbed completely, ensuring a pain-free experience.

### Step 3: Cleaning the Infection

The dentist removes infected tissue and cleans the root canals thoroughly.

### Step 4: Sealing the Tooth

The canals are filled and sealed to prevent future infection.

### Step 5: Crown Placement

A dental crown is placed to protect the treated tooth and restore normal function.

## Is Root Canal Treatment Painful?

This is one of the most common concerns among patients. Thanks to modern techniques and anesthesia, root canal treatment today is:

* Mostly painless
* Comfortable during the procedure
* Followed by only mild soreness for a few days

Most patients report that RCT feels similar to getting a regular dental filling.

## Importance of a Dental Crown After Root Canal

After RCT, the tooth becomes more fragile. A crown is recommended to protect it.

### Benefits of Crown Placement

* Restores strength and durability
* Prevents tooth fracture
* Improves appearance
* Extends the life of the treated tooth

Skipping the crown can increase the risk of tooth damage over time.
